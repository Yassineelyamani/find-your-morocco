Exploring Chefchaouen: The Blue Pearl

Everything you need to know about visiting Morocco's famous blue city, from the best photo spots to local crafts.

January 25, 2024 · 7 min read

Nestled in the Rif Mountains of northern Morocco, Chefchaouen is one of the country's most photogenic destinations. Known as the "Blue Pearl," this charming city is famous for its powder-blue buildings, winding medina streets, and relaxed atmosphere that feels worlds away from the bustling cities.

Why is Chefchaouen Blue?

The tradition of painting buildings blue dates back to the 15th century when Jewish refugees settled in Chefchaouen. The blue represents the sky and heaven, serving as a reminder to live a spiritual life. Today, the blue walls create a magical atmosphere that attracts photographers and travelers from around the world.

Best Photo Spots in Chefchaouen

Outa el Hammam Square

The heart of the medina, this square is surrounded by blue-washed buildings and features the iconic Kasbah. It's the perfect starting point for your exploration and offers excellent photo opportunities throughout the day.

The Spanish Mosque

Perched on a hill overlooking the city, the Spanish Mosque offers panoramic views of Chefchaouen's blue rooftops against the backdrop of the Rif Mountains. Visit at sunset for truly magical lighting.

Ras El Ma Waterfall

A short hike from the city center leads to this beautiful waterfall. The surrounding area offers peaceful walking trails and stunning natural scenery that contrasts beautifully with the blue city.

What to Buy in Chefchaouen

Local Crafts

Chefchaouen is known for its wool blankets, woven baskets, and traditional pottery. The city's artisans produce high-quality goods that make perfect souvenirs and gifts.

Goat Cheese

The surrounding region is famous for its goat cheese. Visit local markets to sample and purchase this delicious specialty, often flavored with herbs or honey.

Traditional Clothing

The region produces distinctive woven garments and djellabas in traditional patterns. These make unique, authentic souvenirs of your visit.

Best Time to Visit Chefchaouen

Spring (April-May) and fall (September-October) offer the most pleasant weather for exploring the city. Summer can be quite warm, though the mountain location provides some relief. Winter brings cooler temperatures and occasional rain, but the blue city takes on a mystical quality in the mist.

How to Get to Chefchaouen

Chefchaouen is located in northern Morocco, about 2 hours from Fes and 3.5 hours from Tangier. The most convenient way to visit is as part of a private Morocco tour that includes transportation and a knowledgeable guide.

Where to Stay

The city offers charming riads and guesthouses within the medina walls, as well as modern hotels just outside. Staying in the medina allows you to experience the blue city's magic around the clock, with early morning and evening being particularly peaceful for photography.
