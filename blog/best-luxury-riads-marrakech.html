Best Luxury Riads in Marrakech

Our curated selection of the finest riads in Marrakech, from intimate boutique properties to palatial retreats.

January 20, 2024 · 6 min read

Marrakech, the Red City, is home to some of Morocco's most exquisite traditional accommodations. Riads—traditional Moroccan houses with interior courtyards—offer an authentic yet luxurious experience that perfectly complements your Morocco journey.

What Makes a Riad Special?

Riads are architectural masterpieces designed around central courtyards, often featuring intricate tilework, carved cedar, and serene fountains. These hidden gems provide an oasis of calm amidst the bustling medina, offering privacy and tranquility that hotels simply cannot match.

Our Top Riad Recommendations

Riad Fes

A stunning example of traditional Moroccan craftsmanship, this riad features hand-carved cedar ceilings, intricate zellige tilework, and a rooftop terrace with panoramic views of the medina. The intimate atmosphere and personalized service make it perfect for couples seeking romance.

Royal Mansour

For those seeking the ultimate in luxury, Royal Mansour offers private riads with their own pools, butler service, and world-class spa. Located near the royal palace, this property redefines the concept of traditional riad accommodation.

La Sultana

A collection of five interconnected riads forming a boutique hotel, La Sultana offers the perfect blend of authenticity and modern comfort. Each riad has its own character, with some featuring private pools and all offering exceptional service.

Choosing the Right Riad for Your Stay

Location Matters

Consider whether you prefer to stay in the heart of the medina for easy access to souks and restaurants, or in the quieter Hivernage district for more space and modern amenities. Both areas offer unique advantages.

Amenities to Look For

  • Rooftop terraces with Atlas Mountain views
  • Traditional hammam and spa facilities
  • On-site restaurants serving authentic Moroccan cuisine
  • Private courtyards and gardens
  • Air conditioning (essential for summer months)

Best Time to Stay in Marrakech Riads

Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) offer the most pleasant weather for enjoying riad courtyards and terraces. Summer can be quite hot, though many riads have excellent cooling systems and refreshing pools.
